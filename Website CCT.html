Conditional Cash Transfer: Is it an answer to breaking out of poverty?

While replicating the evaluation of the Conditional Cash Transfer (CCT) program in Mexico, we were very curious how these programs performed elsewhere in the world. Since, we couldn't find publicly available data for other studies to work with, we ended up building this website to serve as a small repository for a meta-analysis of CCT around the world. We have referenced this paper for our work. Of all the countries studied in the paper, we thought it would be a good idea to pick three countries representing different regions in the world. Thus, we ended up picking Brazil, Kenya, and Bangladesh.

Hope you'd enjoy reading about CCT as we did while working for this project!

CCT Outcomes in Brazil

Brazil's CCT program Bolsa Familia is also one of the most successful CCT programs across the globe. It started early and evolved over the years.

The current program is very similar to Mexican CCT program, the evaluation of which we replicated for our project. Other facts abouut the program are listed in the table:

CCT Results
Scope of CCT Nation wide program serving 11 million people
Conditional On Education and health outcomes
CCT's Impact Unlike, the Mexican CCT program, Brazil's CCT didn't include an evaluation in its design. Thus, very less is known about its impact on consumption, poverty, and other development indicators.

CCT outcomes in Kenya

Kenya's CCT program focusses on improving the primary education outcomes. It especially serves the orphans and vulnerable children to help them cope with the crisis in the wake of HIV/AIDS. It is interesting to note that Kenya is among the few countries where CCT prompted developing better poverty maps/households targeting system.

Quick Facts about Jaipur:

CCT Results
Scope of CCT Pilot program serving few thousands of families
Conditional On Cash transfers on orphans and vulnerables
Major Findings An evaluation of the program found that though the CCT was not intended to affect sexual behavior change, it nonetheless reduced the risk of HIV in young people in beneficiary households by 23 percent!

CCT outcomes in Bangladesh

Bangladesh's CCT program especially focusses on education program. Again, its focus within education is also very narrow. It specifically looks into secondary education. The main objective of this program was to narrow the gender gap in education.

Other facts about the program in Bangladesh are listed in the table:

CCT Results
Scope of CCT Has a regional focus, not serving the entire country
Conditional On Female secondary assistance program
Major Findings Impact evaluation of the program has showed that the CCT was successful in lowering the gender gap by 12 percentage points.
